Environment of a fern

The ferns habitat is a rainforest. The rainforest environment is hot, moist and humid.
There is a lot of soil in the rainforest. the exact amount of soil in a tropical rainforest is 48% and in a Temperate Deciduous Forest is 69% soil.
Rainforest have a lot of water. That is why it is called a rainforest. Rainforests are known to have some of the largest rivers ever.
The average temperature of a rainforest is around 27 degrees celsius and it rarely drops below 18 degrease celsius and rarely rises above 34 degrees celsius.
Rainforest have a lot of heavy rain. the amount of rain is about 2,000 mm of rainfall and in some arias the mount of rainfall is 10,920 mm.
Rainforest receive 12 hours of sunlight daily but less then 2% of the light acutely reaches the ground. This is because there is so many plants in a rainforst.

Adaptations of a fern

You know that a plant needs water to survive, but a fern needs a lot of water like a cati. if you touch the leaves of a leather leaf fern you will notice that they wave really waxy leaves like the stem of a cacti. They are used to reduce water loss especily in dry weather
Some plants don't just have physical adaptations (like the one you just red) but some have the ability to work with other plants or organisms.
This ability is called symbiosis. This is used by some ferns to work with the fungi called Mycorrhizae. These fungi help ferns to collect more water and even act like a medicine to help protect ferns from the bad things in the soil. In return the fungi gets extra sugar from the ferns.
Another special features of a fern is that they have fibrous roots that let them easily absorb water and nutrients from the soil to insure successful growth
The amount of water a fern needs weekly is incredible. Ferns need 25.4 to 50.8 mm of water a week.
